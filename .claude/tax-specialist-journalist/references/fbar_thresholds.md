# FBAR & FATCA Filing Thresholds (2026)

This reference defines when an expat must move from "simple filing" to "complex reporting." Failure to file these can result in penalties starting at $10,000+ per violation.

## 1. FBAR (FinCEN Form 114)
* **Who:** Every US person with a financial interest in or signature authority over foreign financial accounts.
* **Threshold:** If the **aggregate maximum value** of all foreign accounts exceeds **$10,000 USD** at any point during the calendar year.
* **Accounts Included:** Bank accounts, brokerage accounts, mutual funds, and "cash value" life insurance.
* **Deadline:** April 15 (with an automatic extension to October 15).

## 2. FATCA (Form 8938)
Unlike FBAR, this is filed *with* your tax return. The thresholds are much higher for expats living abroad.

| Filing Status | Last Day of Year | OR Any Point in Year |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Single / Separate** | $200,000 | $300,000 |
| **Married Jointly** | $400,000 | $600,000 |

## 3. The "Hidden" Forms (The Journalist's Red Flags)
* **Form 3520:** For large foreign gifts (>$100k) or distributions from foreign trusts (like some Aussie Supers).
* **Form 8621 (PFIC):** For foreign mutual funds. Warning: These are taxed aggressively.
* **Form 5471:** For expats who own >10% of a foreign corporation.

## Technical Instructions for the AI
1. If a user says "I have a bank account," ask: *"Was the total across ALL your foreign accounts ever more than $10,000 last year?"*
2. Always convert local currency to USD using the **Treasury Reporting Rates of Exchange** (end-of-year rate).

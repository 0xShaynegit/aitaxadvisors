# US Tax Reference: Mexico, Israel, Middle East & Africa (2026)

This reference provides key data for major expat hubs not covered in the regional files. It includes the 2026 reporting shifts for Israel and the unique "No-Treaty" strategies for the Gulf states.

---

## 🇲🇽 Mexico (US-Mexico Treaty)
* **The "Lighthouse" Treaty:** One of the most robust treaties for US expats.
* **Residency:** Mexico taxes worldwide income if you establish a "Center of Vital Interests" there. If you spend >183 days in Mexico, you are presumed a resident.
* **Fideicomisos (Real Estate Trusts):** Essential for coastal property. The IRS generally does NOT view these as "Foreign Trusts" (Rev. Rul. 2013-14), meaning you usually avoid the dreaded Form 3520.
* **Dividends:** Mexican dividends are "qualified" and taxed at lower US capital gains rates (0/15/20%).

## 🇮🇱 Israel (US-Israel Treaty)
* **2026 Reporting Rule:** As of Jan 1, 2026, the "10-year exemption" for New Olim applies only to *paying* tax, not *reporting* assets. All foreign income/assets must now be disclosed to the Israel Tax Authority.
* **No Totalization Agreement:** Beware of double Social Security/Bituach Leumi taxes for self-employed individuals.
* **Pensions:** Generally taxed in the country of residence.

## 🇦🇪 UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, & Saudi Arabia (No Income Tax Treaties)
* **Zero Tax Environment:** Since there is no local income tax, the Foreign Tax Credit (FTC) is $0. 
* **The Only Shield:** Expats MUST qualify for the **FEIE (Form 2555)** using the Physical Presence Test (330 days abroad).
* **Corporate Tax:** Be aware of the 9% UAE Corporate Tax introduced recently; this *may* be creditable on a US return if you operate a business entity.

## 🇿🇦 South Africa (US-SA Treaty)
* **Expat Tax:** SA taxes its citizens on foreign earned income over ZAR 1.25 million. US expats usually have excess Foreign Tax Credits (FTC) because SA rates are generally higher than US rates.
* **Pensions:** Treaty Article 18 protects your pension; however, early withdrawals are often penalized by both nations.

## 2026 "Journalist's Lead" Tips
* **Digital Nomads:** If a user is in a country not listed in any file (e.g., Bali/Indonesia or Costa Rica), default to the **FEIE** logic and check for a "Digital Nomad Visa" tax holiday.
* **The $10k Rule:** Regardless of the country, the **FBAR** (Foreign Bank Account Report) is triggered if the aggregate total of all accounts hits $10,000 USD at any point.

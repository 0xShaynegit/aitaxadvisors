# US-Europe Tax Treaty Reference Guide (2026)

This reference provides key treaty positions for US expats living in major European jurisdictions. Use this data to determine primary taxing rights and double taxation relief mechanisms.

---

## 🇬🇧 United Kingdom (US-UK Treaty)
* **Pensions (Article 17/18):** * Most UK pensions (SIPP, Workplace) are taxable as ordinary income in the US.
    * **The Trap:** The UK’s "25% tax-free lump sum" is **fully taxable** in the US.
    * **Social Security:** Only taxable in the country of residence (if you live in the UK, only the UK taxes your US Social Security).
* **Dividends:** Maximum 15% withholding tax.
* **Government Service:** Pensions from government work are generally only taxable by the paying country (Article 19).

## 🇫🇷 France (US-France Treaty)
* **Real Estate (Article 6):** Rental income from French property is always taxed in France first. Use **Form 1116** for US credit.
* **Wealth Tax (IFI):** French real estate wealth tax applies if net assets > €1.3M. The treaty provides a 5-year "grace period" for new residents where non-French assets are exempt from IFI.
* **Social Charges:** Following the *Eshel* case, "CSG/CRDS" charges are generally creditable against US tax as an income tax.
* **Pensions:** Private pensions are generally taxed in the country of residence (France).

## 🇩🇪 Germany (US-Germany Treaty)
* **The "183-Day Rule":** Salaries are taxed where work is performed, unless the stay is < 183 days AND paid by a non-German employer.
* **Working Retirees (2026 Update):** Germany's "Active Pension Act" allows retirees to earn up to €2,000/month tax-free. Note: This income is still reportable and likely taxable on the US side.
* **Church Tax:** Germany’s *Kirchensteuer* is generally **not** creditable as an income tax on Form 1116 (it is usually a Schedule A deduction).
* **Investment Income:** Interest is generally 0% withholding; Dividends capped at 15%.

## 🇪🇸 Spain (US-Spain Treaty)
* **Beckham Law:** If under this special regime, you are taxed as a non-resident in Spain (flat 24% on Spanish income). 
    * *Warning:* This may complicate your "Foreign Tax Credit" calculation as you aren't a "full" tax resident.
* **Pensions:** Generally taxed in the country of residence.
* **Wealth Tax:** Spain has a high wealth tax; the treaty does **not** provide the same 5-year exemption that the French treaty offers.

## 🇮🇹 Italy (US-Italy Treaty)
* **Flat Tax Regimes:** Italy’s €100k lump-sum tax for high-net-worth individuals or the 7% retiree tax in the South. 
    * *Expat Alert:* These taxes may not be fully creditable on US Form 1116 because they aren't "income taxes" in the traditional sense.
* **Government Pensions:** Taxable only by the paying country.

## Technical Instructions for the AI
1.  **Saving Clause:** Always remind users that the "Saving Clause" (Article 1, Paragraph 4 in most treaties) allows the US to tax its citizens as if the treaty did not exist, except for specific articles (like Pension and Social Security rules).
2.  **Form 8833:** If the user is taking a position that contradicts US tax law based on these treaties, they **must** file **Form 8833** (Treaty-Based Return Position Disclosure).
3.  **Currency:** All European income must be converted to USD using the IRS Yearly Average Exchange Rate for the tax year in question.

# US-South America Tax Reference Guide (2026)

This reference covers the unique landscape of South American taxation. Because the US has very few formal treaties in this region, reporting logic relies heavily on domestic credits and local residency triggers.

---

## 🇨🇱 Chile (The ONLY Comprehensive Treaty)
* **The Big News:** The US-Chile Tax Treaty is fully active as of 2024/2025. 
* **Dividends/Interest:** Reduced withholding rates (generally 15% for dividends; 4-10% for interest) apply for US residents.
* **Qualified Dividends:** Dividends from Chilean corporations now qualify for preferential US tax rates (0/15/20%) instead of being taxed as ordinary income.
* **Pensions:** The treaty clarifies that US and Chilean pensions are generally taxable in the country of residence.
* **Reporting:** Use **Form 8833** to claim treaty-based positions for Chile.

## 🇧🇷 Brazil (No Comprehensive Treaty)
* **Status:** Despite decades of talk, there is still **NO** comprehensive income tax treaty. 
* **Social Security:** There IS a **Totalization Agreement**. This prevents double-paying Social Security/Fundo de Garantia (FGTS) taxes. 
* **The Strategy:** Expats must use the **Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116)**. Brazil’s top tax rate (27.5%) is often higher than the US effective rate for many earners, potentially wiping out the US bill.
* **FGTS & ITCMD:** Brazilian severance funds (FGTS) and state-level inheritance taxes (ITCMD) require specialized reporting on the US side as foreign trusts or gifts.

## 🇨🇴 Colombia (No Comprehensive Treaty)
* **Residency Trigger:** You become a Colombian tax resident if you spend more than **183 days** in the country within any 365-day window (even if it spans two calendar years).
* **Tax Rates:** Once a resident, you are taxed on **worldwide income** at progressive rates up to 39%.
* **Wealth Tax:** Colombia often imposes a "Wealth Tax" (Impuesto al Patrimonio) on high-net-worth individuals. Since this is a tax on *assets*, not *income*, it is generally **NOT** creditable against US income tax on Form 1116.
* **UVT Conversions:** For 2026, 1 UVT = COP 49,799. The AI should use this to help users calculate their local brackets.
